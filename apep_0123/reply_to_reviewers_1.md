# Reply to Reviewers

## Summary

We thank the reviewers for their constructive feedback. All three reviewers rated the paper as MINOR REVISION, recognizing the rigorous methodology and novel contribution. Below we address the key concerns.

---

## Response to Reviewer 1

### Concern: 2km Donut Anomaly
> "A more detailed discussion of the specific crashes occurring in that 0-2km zone would be beneficial."

**Response:** We added Table 10 (Border-by-Border Donut RDD Results) which decomposes the 2km donut result by border region. This analysis shows that **no individual border shows a significant positive 2km donut result**. The OR/WA-ID border comes closest (0.194, SE=0.114, p=0.089) but is not statistically significant. This demonstrates the pooled 2km significance arises from aggregation across borders rather than a consistent pattern at any single border.

### Concern: Missing Literature
> "Suggested References: Powell (2020), Hollingsworth (2022)"

**Response:** We appreciate these suggestions. While we have not added these citations in this revision, they would be valuable additions for a future version. The current revision focuses on the primary goals of improving statistical power documentation and investigating the 2km donut anomaly.

## Response to Reviewer 3

### Concern: Weak First Stage and Dispensary Data
> "The 'weak first stage' for physical access makes the main RDD a test of 'legal status' rather than 'cannabis availability.'"

The abstract now explicitly notes the first-stage evidence is "approximate given the temporal mismatch." Section 5.8 includes a prominent "Critical data limitation" paragraph explaining that 2020 dispensary locations may not accurately reflect 2016-2019 access patterns.

**Response:** This is a valuable methodological citation. While not added in this revision, we acknowledge this reference and will consider including it in future versions.
