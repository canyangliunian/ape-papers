## Reviewer 1 (GPT-5-mini): MAJOR REVISION

**1. Instrument exclusion validity**
We added placebo shock tests showing GDP-weighted and employment-weighted instruments are null (p>0.10 for both), directly supporting the exclusion restriction (Section 10.6). Distance-restricted instruments with improved balance (Table 5) further address this concern.

**2. SCI timing (2018 vintage)**
We added a dedicated discussion in Section 11.5 noting that SCI reflects long-run connections (Bailey et al. 2018), our shift-share framework treats SCI as pre-determined shares (Borusyak et al. 2022), and our IRS migration analysis finds no endogenous migration response.

**3. Pre-trend evidence**
We expanded the pre-trend sensitivity discussion (Section 10.7) to honestly acknowledge the limited pre-period (QWI begins 2012Q1) and the non-trivial 2012 coefficient. We note that our confidence rests on three complementary pieces of evidence: placebo shocks, distance-restricted instruments, and AR confidence sets.

**4. Shock contribution transparency**
Table 7 reports shock contributions with HHI=0.08 (effective N~12 shocks). Joint state exclusion (Section 10.5) shows stability when removing CA+NY jointly and CA+NY+WA jointly.

**5. Mechanism evidence**
We acknowledge earnings results are noisy and add a discussion in Section 11.5 explaining composition effects: if network information brings more low-wage workers into employment, average county earnings may not rise.
