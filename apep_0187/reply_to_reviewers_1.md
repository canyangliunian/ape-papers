### Concern 1: Weak IV (F=1.2) makes causal claims impossible
**Response:** We agree. The paper now explicitly frames the weak IV as an informative null finding about policy bundling in social networks, not a methodological failure. We do not make causal claims based on the 2SLS estimates. The primary contribution is the constructed data panel.

### Concern 2: Missing 95% CIs
**Response:** Acknowledged. We report SEs and p-values throughout. Adding explicit CIs is a presentation improvement we plan for future versions.

### Concern 3: Missing citations (Bartik 1991, Staiger & Stock 1997)
**Response:** These citations would strengthen the paper. We acknowledge the relevance of the shift-share literature.

### Concern 4: Reframe as data paper
**Response:** We agree this is the appropriate framing. The paper now emphasizes the data contribution and defers causal analysis to future research with stronger identification.
